    What is speech impediment?


     Speech impediment, or speech disorder, happens when a persons mouth, jaw, tongue and vocal tract 
     cannot work together to produce words, making it difficult for others to understand them. In some cases,
     a speech impediment is a sign of physical or developmental differences.If untreated, speech impediment 
     can make it difficult to learn to read and write.

    Causes for speech impediments

            
   There's different causes for speech impediments. An example would be stuttering, which might be a sign of a developmental 
   delay, or it could be hereditary or it could happen because a child's brain can't coordinate the functions that help them to speak.
   Tongue-tie, on the other hand, is a physical condition that makes it hard for your child to move their tongue.
   Speech impediments can also indicate an unrelated underlying condition, such as:
